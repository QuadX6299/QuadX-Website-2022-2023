South Africa FTC Camp
7/4/2019 - 7/11/2019

After mentoring FLL teams in South Africa through Skype during the 2018-2019 season, we decided we needed to bring higher levels of robotics to them directly. A huge thanks to our sponsors AMD, GoBilda, AndyMark, E2 Open, and HP.

Day 1

For the first day of our FTC Camp in South Africa, we introduced the kids of Tzaneen to the core of FTC. We taught the very essentials of hardware, software, and marketing to the subteams with worksheets, videos, and demonstrations. Finally, we showcased the game they will be playing for their rookie camp season, QuadX's Gold Rush!

Hardware
      Students that chose hardware as their speciality learned about types of drivetrains and intakes, torque and speed interactions, gear ratio calculations, and all fundamental parts in FTC like build plates and sprockets. Each person got their own workbook packed with information and some examples from past years that can be referenced during the season. They also started AND finished two mecanum chassis that will be the basis for their robot in Gold Rush.
Software
      Software students began their camp by learning the groundwork of the Java language. They were introduced to essentials such as variables and their types, loops and their uses, as well as method organization.
Marketing
      In the marketing speciality, students were introduced to the main aspects of marketing such as community outreach and team identity. They began learning about how to write an effective business plan and brainstorming logos and team names.
