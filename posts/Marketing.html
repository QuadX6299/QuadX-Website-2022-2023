Marketing/PR
1/2/2019

Probably the least recognized part of robotics, nevertheless, vital to success.

1. What is marketing?

Marketing is more of the “business oriented” side of FIRST. Our Intro to Marketing and Public relations can be found on our website, or here!

2. What software do you use to make all these cheat sheets?

Mostly just Adobe InDesign! The real hard part is organizing the content and even coming up with ideas to make them about. Then it's just down to organizing and making your content visually appealing, which takes more tries than you would think.

Our Twitter homepage.
3. Why even use Twitter?

Not only does Twitter connect us to teams across the globe, it's fun to use. Twitter is widely used by other high tech companies and the business industry across the globe. But truthfully, we like to look back three or four years later at the horrible pictures of us we posted :).

Some hardware members working in the bow tie assembly line.
4. How do you get the rest of your team (ie, hardware/software) to participate in Marketing activities?

It can be really hard sometimes, I won't lie. But, if you find you're having troubles with getting others involved in marketing, have a sit down team meeting where you and your team lead emphasize that Marketing is still a pivotal part of being in FIRST. Generally, I have my marketing team handle the true “marketing items”, like Pit setup, poster design, and outreach organization, and try to incorporate the other subteam members into things like pit setup, poster/prop assembly, and even our bowtie making shop.
